Pan-Seared Salmon Citrus Salad

 
Ingredients Optional Citrus Vinaigrette*
 
1 Tbsp coconut oil ¼ cup olive oil
1 lb fresh salmon fillets ¼ cup red wine vinegar
⅛ tsp sea salt ¼ cup orange, lemon, or lime juice
⅛ tsp black pepper 1 tsp Dijon mustard
1 Tbsp lemon juice (about ½ a lemon) ½ Tbsp Stevia®
8 cups spring mix salad greens (about 12 oz) 1 tsp sea salt
5 diced Roma tomatoes ½ tsp black pepper
1 diced cucumber *Optional ingredients are not included in nutritional calculations
1 oz plain rice crackers, crumbled
¼ cup Feta cheese crumbles
 
Directions
 
1. Place salmon on large plate. Drizzle lemon juice evenly over fillets. Sprinkle with salt and pepper.
2. Heat a large iron skillet to medium-high heat. Add coconut oil. When melted, add salmon fillets skin side down and immediately reduce heat to medium-low. (If you have a splatter guard, I recommend placing it on top at this time.)
3. Pan-sear salmon for 7 mintues, flip and fry for 1 additional minute or until center reads 145 degress. (Cooking time might vary depending on the thickness of each fillet.) Remove from heat and set aside.
4. In a small mixing bowl, combine all Citrus Vinaigrette ingredients and whisk until thoroughly mixed. Set aside.
5. In a large bowl, toss greens, diced tomatoes and cucumbers.
6. Assemble salads: place 2 cups of tossed greens on a large plate or bowl. Top with 4 oz of warm salmon chunks, then 2 Tbsp rice crackers and 1 Tbsp feta. Drizzle with (optional) 3 Tbsp of Citrus Vinaigrette.
 
Calories: 384 Per Serving (2 cups of tossed greens, 4 oz of salmon, 2 Tbsp rice crackers, and 1 Tbsp feta)
